The deceased appointed his second spouse as estate trustee. His children from a prior relationship contest both the appointment and the residue distribution, citing undue influence and lack of testamentary capacity.

The court found that the testator had capacity, but that significant financial provision was necessary for a dependent adult child who had not been explicitly named in the will.
